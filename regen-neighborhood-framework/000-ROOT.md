# ROOT — Regen Tribe Knowledge System

Regen Tribe builds the ecosystem that accelerates Regenerative Neighborhoods.

A Regenerative Neighborhood closes loops.
Water. Food. Energy. Waste.
It builds trust.
It builds shared memory.
It replicates.

## The Five Levels

Every system fits one of five levels.

Level 1 is the Cell.
One person.
One home.
One skill.
The smallest unit that can act.

Level 2 is the Tissue.
Three to twelve people.
A household or a core team.
Trust forms here.
Small groups share and decide.

Level 3 is the Organ.
Twelve to 200 people.
One neighborhood.
Shared infrastructure.
Local governance.
The scale where collective action works.

Level 4 is the System.
A bioregion.
Multiple neighborhoods.
Trade. Federation.
Distances that need transport or communication.

Level 5 is the Body.
The whole planetary network.
Knowledge commons.
Standards that let bioregions talk.
Shared infrastructure at global scale.

The five levels give every system a size and scope.

## The Four Domains

Every system spans four domains.

Domain A is Physical.
What you build.
How you build it.
What it costs.

Domain B is Social.
Who does what.
How decisions get made.
How trust grows.
How conflict gets resolved.

Domain C is Information.
What you know.
How you know it.
How memory persists.
How sensing happens.

Domain D is Exchange.
What moves between people and places.
Value. Currency. Energy. Knowledge.
How trade happens without extraction.

The four domains give every system a type.

The five levels and four domains form a grid.
Every project occupies one cell in this grid.
The work is to fill the grid with working systems.

## Anti-Capture Principle

Every system must resist four types of capture.

Capital capture means wealth leaving the community.
Prevention: circulation over compounding.
Surplus flows back in.

Narrative capture means attention serving outside interests.
Prevention: commons over platform.
Shared story stays local.

Identity capture means people sorted into scores.
Prevention: contribution over score.
What you do matters.

Governance capture means rules set by outsiders.
Prevention: exit-friendly design.
Leaving must be easy.

## Technology Philosophy

Technology serves life.
Not the other way around.
Open tools. Community owned hardware.
No royalties. No lock-in.

The design method is constraint-based.
You build by constraining.
Not by adding.
The wall is the design.
The bounded space is the feature.

The architecture is compositional.
Small parts.
Large behaviors.
Nodes compose into workflows.
Beliefs compose into knowledge.
Roles compose into governance.

Formation Coding Language (FCL) sends ideas through space.
FCL uses geometry.
Geometry is universal.
All intelligent systems understand geometry.

## The Gift Economy Principle

No debt.
No rent.
No interest.
Value flows because it can.
Contribution is visible.
Extraction is visible.
Both get named.
