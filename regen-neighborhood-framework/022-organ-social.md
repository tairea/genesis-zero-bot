# The Organ — Social Domain

An organ is one neighborhood.
Twelve to 200 people.

Governance at this scale needs structure.
Not hierarchy.
Structure.

The council holds the decisions.
Each tissue sends one representative.
The council meets once per week.

The council handles four things.
Infrastructure. Disputes. Planning. Resources.

Infrastructure decisions answer these questions.
What to build.
How to maintain it.
Who pays with what.

Disputes means conflicts between tissues.
The council mediates.
It does not judge.

Planning means the council sets the quarterly direction.
The tissues execute.

Resources means the council allocates shared resources.
Tools. Land. Water. Energy.

Consent still rules.
A decision passes when no council member blocks it.

Each council member can block on behalf of their tissue.
This is their job.

The council has a guide.
Not a chair.
The guide holds the process.

Roles at the organ level include these.
Infrastructure lead. Food lead. Energy lead. Education lead.

Each role has a charter.
Purpose defines why the role exists.
Scope defines what it covers.
Authority defines what it can decide.
Accountability defines who reviews it.

The council reviews each role every quarter.

Accountability means this.
Show your work.
Explain your decisions.
Accept feedback.

Land is held in common.
No one person owns the land.
The organ holds the title.

Buildings are owned by the people who built them.
Or by the organ.
This is decided case by case.

When someone leaves their building can be bought by the organ.
Or removed.
Or passed to family.
The decision is made case by case.
There is no fixed rule.

What matters is this.
The land stays in the commons.
The knowledge stays in the commons.

The commons is protected by the council.
The council is guided by consent.

Conflict at the organ level means two tissues disagree.
The council mediates.

If mediation fails an external mediator helps.
Someone from outside the neighborhood.
The external mediator does not decide.
They help the council decide.

Communication works this way.
Each tissue posts a weekly update.
What they did.
What they will do.

Decision logs are public.
Anyone can read them.
This prevents power concentration.

Open records are the anti-capture mechanism.
Hidden decisions enable capture.
