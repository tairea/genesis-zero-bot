# The Tissue — Social Domain

A tissue is a small group.
Three to twelve people.
A household or a core team.

Trust is the foundation.
Everything else builds on it.

Trust grows through repeated interaction.
You do what you say.
Others see.

The first trust act is keeping a small promise.
Nothing more.

Roles emerge from what people actually do.
Not from what they say they will do.

Assign roles by observation.
Watch who does what well.
Ask them to do more of it.

Consent is the decision method.
A decision is made when no one blocks it.

Blocking is serious.
It means the proposed action would harm the blocker.

Blocking requires a proposal everyone can live with.
Not everyone loves it.
Just lives with it.

Consent is not agreement.
Agreement means yes.
Consent means no blocking.

Time banking measures contribution.
One hour of work equals one time credit.
All work counts.
Cooking counts.
Cleaning counts.
Repair counts.
Teaching counts.
Listening counts.

Time credits do not earn interest.
This is the rule.
No exceptions.

Time credits have a floor.
You never go below zero.
The community tops you up.

Time credits have a ceiling.
You cannot accumulate beyond 200 hours per year.
The ceiling prevents hoarding.
The floor prevents poverty.

Conflict is normal.
It is a signal.
It means something needs attention.

The conflict process has steps.
Name the issue.
Hear all sides.
Propose a solution.
Test it.

If the conflict stays unresolved get a third party.
Someone outside the group.
The third party does not decide.
They help the group decide.

Meetings have a format.
Check-in round. Agenda. Discussion. Decision. Check-out round.

Check-in means each person says how they feel.
One sentence.

Check-out means each person says what they got from the meeting.
One sentence.

This format takes 15 minutes.
It prevents most meeting problems.

Decision logs are written.
Who decided what.
When.
Why.
This is memory.

Memory prevents the same arguments from happening again and again.
When someone leaves the group keeps functioning.
The decision logs remain.
This is the key advantage over informal groups.
Knowledge persists.

A tissue has a guide.
Not a leader.
The guide holds the process.
The guide does not make decisions.
The guide makes sure decisions get made well.
