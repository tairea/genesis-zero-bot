# ASD-STE100 Part 1 Writing Rules (Sections 1-9)

## 1. Words

- Use approved words only from ASD-STE100 dictionary
- Do not use jargon, slang, or colloquialisms
- Use simple, direct words
- One meaning per word (avoid polysemes)
- Compound nouns: use approved forms only

## 2. Multi-Word Nouns

- Noun clusters limited to 3 words maximum
- Use hyphenation for compound modifiers
- Approved combinations only
- Example: "water tank level" (3 words OK)

## 3. Verbs

- Use approved verbs from dictionary only
- One tense per verb (present tense preferred)
- Use active voice
- Avoid hidden verbs (convert "make a determination" to "determine")
- Imperative mood for procedures

## 4. Sentences

- Maximum 20 words for procedural sentences
- Maximum 25 words for descriptive sentences
- One idea per sentence
- Simple sentence structure preferred
- Avoid subordinate clauses where possible

## 5. Procedural Writing

- Use numbered steps
- One action per step
- State the purpose before the procedure
- Include warnings and cautions where needed
- Use consistent command verbs

## 6. Descriptive Writing

- State the subject first
- Use comparative language sparingly
- Include measurements and specifications
- Avoid vague quantifiers ("many", "few", "some")
- Be specific and concrete

## 7. Safety Instructions

- Use standard warning markers: CAUTION, WARNING, DANGER
- State the hazard, not just the consequence
- Give clear preventive actions
- Keep warnings prominent and separate
- Follow hierarchy: DANGER > WARNING > CAUTION

## 8. Punctuation

- Use periods at end of sentences
- Use colons sparingly
- Use lists for multiple items
- Avoid em-dashes and non-ASCII characters
- Use consistent punctuation throughout

## 9. Writing Practices

- Write at elementary reading level (Grade 8 target)
- Use consistent terminology
- Avoid metaphors and figurative language
- Use approved acronyms only
- Spell out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10+
