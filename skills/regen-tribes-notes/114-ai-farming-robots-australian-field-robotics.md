# AI Farming Robots - Australian Field Robotics

Source: Professor Salah Sukkarieh, Australian Center for Field Robotics, University of Sydney.

Three robots, three scales.

## SWAGBOT - Cattle Farm Hand

4-wheel drive, works with grazing livestock 24/7.

Tests: mingle with herd. Cows curious, not scared. Follow voice commands (Rod's voice recorded). Herd cattle to fresh pasture.

Key insight: Cattle can be trained to follow a robot if food reward is attached.

Future: Monitor individual animal health. Detect sickness. Targeted pest control (African boxthorn).

African boxthorn weed: $40K to clear half a paddock mechanically. Swagbot with spray + weed ID = potential multi-million dollar solution.

Result after 1 month: Cows followed Swagbot's voice recording. One brave cow ate hay off it. Breakthrough moment.

## DIGITAL FARMHAND - Small-Scale Organic Farmer

Modular: swap tools depending on season (sprayer, flame weeder, seeder, vacuum).

Farmer's own phone as the computer. Bluetooth connects phone to robot. No extra cost.

Tests on Evan Anderson's organic farm: Flame weeding worked. Spraying row width mismatch, fixed next day. Vacuum pest removal worked.

Challenge: Unstructured environment. Cannot afford human labor. Needs low cost.

Quote: Farmers cannot afford fancy expensive tech that they are scared to break.

## RIPPER - Large-Scale Commercial Vegetable Robot

Solar powered. Covers 5 acres per day at 6 km/h.

Learns new crops in hours using deep learning. Broccoli = 100 hand-labeled images + overnight training.

Detects pests, foreign objects, weeds. Tags them in real time.

Tools: Precision sprayer, mechanical weeder, vacuum.

Can detect broccoli. Target spray weeds only. Dramatic chemical reduction.

Field trial: 7,000 images collected. Machine learning. Autonomous weed identification.

## AI Architecture

Multiple computers: one processes camera data, one controls wheels, one bridges between them.

Real-time: while moving, absorbs info, understands environment, makes decisions instantaneously.

Learning: supervised (humans label images) then deep learning phase (robot learns without supervision).

Sensory data: RGB cameras plus laser (LiDAR) for distance estimation. Better than human eye.

## Why Australia Leads

Large properties + harsh conditions = forcing function.

Farms are unstructured, huge, variable. Cannot use factory automation. Must solve hard outdoor robotics.

Result: Australia is world leader in field robotics.

## The Farmer Response

Initial fear: Robots taking jobs. Losing human element of stewardship.

After trials: I see opportunity, not replacement. I would have a robot tomorrow.

Rod (cattle farmer): I can see my children having a different approach to robots.

Evan (organic farmer): It does not replace the farmer, it gives me time for more interesting work.

## Strategic Insight for Regen Tribe

This is the exact playbook for construction automation.

1. Start with the hardest outdoor task
2. Make it modular for multiple tools
3. Use the operator's existing phone as computer
4. Keep cost low
5. Learn from farmers, not from factory automation

The same approach applies to construction robots, water system monitors, and infrastructure maintenance.

The unstructured environment is the advantage. Factory automation cannot handle it. Field robotics can.
