## Most Significant Finding: Item 4 - Pass-Through Voting Amendment

**Proposal:** Amend subsidiary Certificate of Incorporation to **REMOVE** pass-through voting provision.

**Vote threshold:** MAJORITY of outstanding shares (higher than routine items). Abstentions count AGAINST.

**What pass-through voting does:** Allows shares held by nominees (brokers, banks) to be voted by the actual beneficial owner.

**What removal means:**
- Broker votes count at broker discretion
- Beneficial owners lose direct voting power
- Management retains more control
- Dispersed shareholders harder to coordinate

This is a **concentration of control** move. BlackRock already has structural advantages from AUM scale. Removing pass-through voting makes it harder for dispersed shareholders to oppose management.

Fink has been **underpaid relative to performance** by MDCC's own standards — suggesting pay is set conservatively to avoid shareholder backlash.
