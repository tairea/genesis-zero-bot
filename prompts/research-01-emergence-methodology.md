# Research Prompt 01: Emergence-Based Change Methodology

## Context

Current frameworks (LJMap and Avancier) model change as engineered: re-prioritize values, or design the target architecture. Complexity science suggests change in complex adaptive systems cannot be engineered — it can only be invited, allowed, or catalyzed.

## Research Questions

### Core Problem
How do you create conditions for emergence in a community system without imposing a predetermined outcome?

### Specific Investigations

**Q1: What distinguishes emergence-invitation from planning?**
- Literature review: Complexity theory, Theory U (Scharmer), Agile/Lean, Cynefin framework
- Find: What are the explicit techniques for "creating conditions" vs. "designing outcomes"?
- Distill: A practical distinction — not philosophical, but operational. What does a facilitator DO differently in each mode?

**Q2: What are the measurable signals that a field is ready to shift?**
- Current LJMap measures values at static points. What would it mean to measure the "energy" or "coherence" of a field?
- Research: phase transitions in social systems, collective consciousness indicators, Field of Trust indicators (from Mythogen/FOT literature)
- Find: Early warning signals that pattern change is imminent in a community

**Q3: How fast must feedback loops run relative to environmental change?**
- Current annual assessments fail because the field moves faster
- Research: feedback loop design in complex systems, cadCAD (complexity simulation), organizational resilience literature
- Find: A method for determining appropriate feedback loop speed given system volatility

**Q4: What is the relationship between individual meaning and collective emergence?**
- LJMap: individual Field of Meaning drives change
- Complex systems: individual change and collective emergence are coupled but not identical
- Research: how do individual meaning-shifts aggregate into collective pattern shifts?
- Find: A model of the micro-macro bridge for values-driven emergence

**Q5: What does "leave the field intact" mean operationally?**
- Every diagnostic or intervention potentially disturbs the field it measures
- Research: observer effect in social systems, second-order cybernetics (von Foerster), contemplative science
- Find: A protocol for intervention that does not contaminate what it measures

## Deliverables

1. **Emergence Invitation Protocol** — A step-by-step facilitation methodology for creating conditions for emergence, distinct from planning or design
2. **Field Readiness Diagnostic** — A set of questions or observable indicators for determining whether a community is ready for emergence invitation vs. structural intervention
3. **Feedback Loop Design Guide** — A method for determining appropriate cadence and type of feedback given community volatility
4. **Micro-Macro Bridge Model** — A conceptual model showing how individual meaning shifts connect to collective pattern emergence
5. **Non-Contamination Protocol** — A practice for ensuring diagnostic interventions don't distort what they measure

## Execution Instructions

Work through each Q separately. For each:
1. Search literature and existing frameworks
2. Synthesize findings into a working definition or protocol
3. Note gaps — where does existing theory fail or go silent?
4. Propose a test: how would you know if this works?

Do not default to technology solutions. The questions are epistemological and methodological first.
