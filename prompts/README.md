## Execution Model

**For AI agents:**
1. Read `synthesis-00-master-research-agenda.md` first
2. Understand your position in the five-stream map
3. Read the relevant research prompt file for your stream
4. Execute the research questions in sequence
5. Cross-reference with other streams before finalizing
6. Document findings to `../memory/YYYY-MM-DD-research-log.md`

**For human coordinators:**
- These prompts are designed to be handed to capable AI agents for execution
- Human role: review outputs, provide community context, make final decisions on trade-offs
- The prompts assume a human can step in at any point and redirect

## Key Constraints (Non-Negotiable)

1. **No black boxes.** Every algorithmic or analytical decision must be traceable to explicit inputs.
2. **Community ownership.** The system must be maintainable by community members, not just by specialists.
3. **Ecological embeddedness.** Non-human stakeholders are first-class, not background context.
4. **Anti-sequential process.** The methodology must be recursive and state-contingent, not linear.
5. **Cultural humility.** No framework claims universality. Every cultural assumption must be labeled.

## Known Gaps (Do Not Fill Without Community Co-Design)

These areas require direct community participation, not just research:
- The indigenous relational values ontology (co-design with indigenous community members)
- The non-human stakeholder representation protocol (co-design with ecological knowledge holders)
- The specific intervention types appropriate for THIS community (not generic)
- The community's own vocabulary for the concepts in the shared ontology glossary

## How to Evaluate Progress

- **Phase 1 complete:** All five research prompts have produced first drafts
- **Phase 2 complete:** Cross-stream integration questions answered, shared ontology glossary created
- **Phase 3 complete:** Minimum viable integration tested in one community context
- **Phase 4 ongoing:** Iterative pilot → feedback → revision cycles

## Provenance

These prompts emerged from a structured analysis:
1. Deep traversal of LJMap.com (values assessment) and Avancier Methods (enterprise architecture)
2. Collision stress testing of both frameworks against opposing viewpoints (Buddhism, indigenous frameworks, complexity theory, Agile, semiotics)
3. Systems requirements extraction (26 requirements across 5 categories)
4. Technology mapping to existing infrastructure (SurrealDB, Genesis Brain, Node-RED, Telegram)

The prompts represent the NEXT layer: what must be built or researched beyond what currently exists.
