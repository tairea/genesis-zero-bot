# Research Prompt 05: Process and Facilitation Methodology

## Context

Both LJMap and Avancier use sequential processes. LJMap: values ranking → Field of Meaning identification → change strategy. Avancier: Initiate → Architect → Plan → Govern → Manage. Both fail at the recursion point: what do you do when the process reveals the process was wrong? The system needs a pool of intervention types with state-contingent selection — not a sequence.

## Research Questions

### Core Problem
Design a community engagement process methodology that is recursive, state-contingent, and non-sequential — where the choice of intervention type depends on the current state of the system, and every phase can return to earlier phases.

### Specific Investigations

**Q1: What are the distinct types of intervention, and what are their assumptions?**
- Current gap: LJMap and Avancier each assume their intervention type is primary
- Research: large group intervention methods (Weisbord & Janoff), Art of Hosting, World Café, Open Space, Future Search, ProAction Café
- Find: A COMPREHENSIVE POOL of intervention types, each with explicit assumptions about the system and what it takes to change it
- Deliverable: INTERVENTION TYPE TAXONOMY — name, assumption, appropriate context, contraindications, facilitation requirements

**Q2: How do you determine which intervention type is appropriate?**
- Currently: practitioners default to what they know
- Research: Cynefin framework (sense-categorize-respond),odal for intervention selection, decision trees in organizational development
- Find: A DIAGNOSTIC PROTOCOL — questions you ask of the system that lead to a specific intervention type recommendation
- Deliverable: INTERVENTION SELECTION DECISION TREE — structured questions that lead to the right intervention type for the current system state

**Q3: What does "recursive process" mean in practice?**
- Avancier's phases are nominally iterative, but in practice: you finish Phase B before starting Phase C
- What would it mean to return to initiation from governance? From within any phase, return to any prior phase?
- Research: recursive awareness (Bateson), double-loop learning (Argyris & Schön), rhizomatic vs. arboreal process models (Deleuze)
- Find: A PROCESS ARCHITECTURE that is genuinely recursive — any phase can restart any other, based on new information
- Deliverable: RECURSIVE PROCESS MODEL — not a linear sequence, a web with entry points and return paths

**Q4: What is the facilitator's role vs. the architect's role?**
- LJMap: coach as values clarifier
- Avancier: architect as system designer
- Both conflate: the person who holds the process is sometimes facilitating emergence, sometimes designing structure
- Research: Process Facilitation (Holman & Devane), the facilitator as a distinct profession,Architecture vs. Facilitation distinction in Open Space/World Café
- Find: A clear distinction between what a facilitator does and what an architect does — and when to switch roles
- Deliverable: FACILITATOR VS. ARCHITECT FRAMEWORK — role definitions, transition signals, ethical boundaries

**Q5: What is "enough" documentation?**
- Avancier's problem: too much documentation becomes static truth
- LJMap's problem: the 6-value report is a social object that catalyzes reflection — what else?
- Research: lightweight architecture (ADRs), living documentation, essential agreements, documentation as conversation artifact
- Find: A PRINCIPLE for determining documentation adequacy — not maximum, not minimum, but WHAT SERVES THE NEXT CONVERSATION
- Deliverable: DOCUMENTATION ADEQUACY CRITERIA — questions that determine how much documentation a given decision or process requires

**Q6: How do you design for community ownership vs. dependency?**
- Both LJMap coaching and Avancier architecture can create dependency: the expert knows, the community implements
- Research: asset-based community development (ABCD), participatory design, co-design principles, community ownership of process
- Find: A PROTOCOL for ensuring the community does not become dependent on the methodology or the facilitator
- Deliverable: OWNERSHIP TRANSFER STRATEGY — how and when the facilitator/external support steps back, and what indicators show the community can carry the process alone

## Deliverables

1. **Intervention Type Taxonomy** — Pool of intervention types with assumptions, contexts, contraindications
2. **Intervention Selection Decision Tree** — Diagnostic questions leading to right intervention type
3. **Recursive Process Model** — Web-like process architecture with entry/return points
4. **Facilitator vs. Architect Framework** — Role definitions, transition signals, ethics
5. **Documentation Adequacy Criteria** — Questions determining documentation level
6. **Ownership Transfer Strategy** — How and when community takes full ownership

## Execution Instructions

Do not produce a new sequential framework. The goal is explicitly ANTI-SEQUENTIAL. Every deliverable should assume the process might be interrupted, redirected, or abandoned at any point — and should be designed accordingly.
