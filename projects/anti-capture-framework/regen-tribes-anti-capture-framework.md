## The Theory of Capture

Four pillars connect into one architecture:

| Pillar | Capture Pattern | Anti-Capture Principle |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|
| Capital | REITs, rent extraction | Circulation over compound |
| Narrative | Algorithm, attention capture | Commons over platform |
| Identity | Scoring, social sorting | Contribution over score |
| Governance | Rules by few, exit hard | Exit-friendly over trap |

**Core insight:** The dharma of wealth capture is that systems capture what they can. The exit is in seeing clearly enough to choose differently.

## Key Threads Explored

### 1. REITs + Tenant Rights
- Rent flows upward into investment vehicles
- Tenant indirectly funds equity investors
- Liability depends on lease + jurisdiction

### 2. Societal MMO
- Society as playable system
- Identity as avatar, governance as game design
- Risk: corporate sovereignty, pay-to-win

### 3. Gamified Democracy
- Continuous participation vs. episodic voting
- XP = reputation, quests = civic actions
- Risk: perverse incentives, surveillance

### 4. X as Prototype
- Gamified attention market, not governance
- Reputation MMO in privately owned arena
- "What happens when you gamify speech but not power?"

### 5. Bugs As Exits
- Dysfunction reveals the mask
- But systems learn and patch bugs
- The exit is in *seeing clearly* — not in the bug itself

### 6. Dharma of Wealth Capture
- Gravity model: wealth pulls more wealth
- Rent loop: extraction without contribution
- The pattern continues because we participate without examining the narrative

## Design Principles for RegenTribes

### Wealth
- **Principle:** Wealth as *tool*, not *score*
- **Mechanic:** Circulation > compound (time-limited holdings, community reinvestment)
- **Test:** Does this create exit options or dependency?

### Narrative
- **Principle:** Stories as *commons*
- **Mechanic:** Open narrative tools, no algorithm manipulation
- **Test:** Can anyone tell their story without being filtered?

### Identity
- **Principle:** Identity as *contribution*, not *measurement*
- **Mechanic:** What you do matters more than what you have
- **Test:** Is this empowering or performative?

### Governance
- **Principle:** Exit is as legitimate as staying
- **Mechanic:** Fork-friendly, data portability, no lock-in
- **Test:** Can someone leave and take their value with them?

### Narrative Control
- **Principle:** Fourth estate as public commons
- **Mechanic:** Open journalism, community-owned media
- **Test:** Who owns the story?

### Choice
- **Principle:** Choice from genuine freedom
- **Mechanic:** Not optimization within the game — but questioning the game itself
- **Test:** Can people choose not to play?
