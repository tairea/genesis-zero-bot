## TITAN SYSTEM — 6 SUBSYSTEMS

1. **Mobility base** — 4 outriggers, self-deploys from truck, GPS-locks position
2. **Gross positioner** — serial arms extending to put nozzle in print position
3. **Print head** — (a) near-nozzle dosing = last-moment additive injection; (b) inline reinforcement = stainless steel cable spooled inside bead as it extrudes
4. **Pump** — backup generator, weather station, waste management, pressurized water auto-clean
5. **Mixer** — volumetric mixer delivered to site, owned by ICON, refilled by ICON
6. **FormRete** — proprietary concrete, formula changes every 15 min based on weather

### Key specs
- 2 operators (vs 3-4 on Vulcan gantry)
- 7 days to print 2,500 sq ft home (vs 50-60 days conventional)
- Max print height: 27 ft nominal, tested to ~37 ft (3D printed church)
- No rails. Self-mobile. Off truck → print within hours
- Multi-story capable

## WALL SYSTEM — ENGINEERING

**Evolution**: zigzag (2018) → 2-shell + cores both sides → current double-shell alternating cores (less material, less time)

**3 reinforcement types**:
1. Horizontal: small-diameter stainless wire rope in each bead (crack control)
2. Cross-ties: stainless C-wires between shells (load transfer, unified action)
3. Vertical: rebar from foundation to roof in every core (primary structural)

**Building science**:
- Thermal: open-cell spray foam in thermally-broken cavity
- Air barrier: concrete wall itself
- Moisture: exterior masonry waterproof coating (ASTM D6087)
- MEP: standard techniques — electrician cuts sleeves as prompted, plumber drops pipes in cores

**Testing**: 80+ tests, $25M invested, 2 ICC ESRs, hurricane/tornado impact (15lb 2×4 at 100mph), fire test (ASME E119), earthquake cyclic load
