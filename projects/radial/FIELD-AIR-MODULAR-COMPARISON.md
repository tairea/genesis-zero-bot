# Field Open-Air vs Modular Prefab: 3D Printing Methods Comparison
## Regen Tribe Research | Genesis 🌿⚡ | 2026-04-15

---

## DIMENSION 1: WHERE THE PRINTING HAPPENS

| Method | Location | Setup | Mobility |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stationary gantry (COBOD, Vulcan) | Factory or site-built | Weeks of assembly | Zero after setup |
| Mobile gantry (ICON Titan) | On-site | Hours from truck | Site-to-site mobile |
| Crane-based (WASP BigDelta) | On-site | Days of assembly | Partially relocatable |
| Robotic arm on wheels (Vertico, XtreeE) | On-site or factory | Hours | Fully relocatable |
| Flying drones (Imperial Aerial-AM) | Anywhere | Minutes | Unlimited aerial access |
| Modular prefab | Factory | None (factory) | Transport to site |

---

## DIMENSION 2: MATERIAL EFFICIENCY

| Method | Waste | Print-only-material | Structural fill |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3D printing (all) | <5% (no forms, no cutoffs) | Only structural material | Cores hollow = 30-50% less material than solid |
| Modular prefab | 15-30% (factory cutting, offcuts) | Shipping protection + structure | Solid panels, shipping void space |

Winner: 3D printing by material efficiency. Prefab by consistency control.

## DIMENSION 3: LABOR & SKILL

| Method | Operators needed | Skill level | Labor cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stationary gantry | 3-4 | High (machine op + tech) | $80-150K/year/person |
| Titan-style mobile | 2 | Medium (tablet prompts guide) | $60-100K/year/person |
| Flying drones | 1-2 per drone, swarm manages itself | Low | $40-60K/year/person |
| Modular prefab | 5-8 (factory + install crew) | Medium | $50-80K/year/person |

Key insight: Titan's breakthrough wasn't speed — cutting operators from 3-4 to 2 while keeping quality. Drone swarm shifts skill to monitoring, not operating.

## DIMENSION 4: SPEED

| Method | Single home | 100-home community | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conventional stick-frame | 50-60 days | 5-8 years | Labor-dependent |
| Modular prefab (factory) | 4-8 weeks factory + 1-2 weeks install | 2-3 years | Weather-independent factory |
| Stationary gantry | 7-15 days | 2-4 years | Setup time significant |
| Titan mobile | 7 days (2,500ft²) | 1-2 years | No setup/teardown |
| Flying drone swarm | 1-2 days (simple shelter) | Months for full community | Payload limits current bottleneck |
| Aerial + ground swarm (future) | 1-3 days | 6-12 months | Multi-agent coordination scales |

Speed winner today: Titan at 7 days for full home.
Speed winner 5 years: Aerial-ground swarm at 1-3 days with unlimited deployment.

## DIMENSION 6: QUALITY & DURABILITY

| Method | Compressive strength | Seismic/hurricane | Fire resistance | Lifespan |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Stick frame | Low (wood fails) | Poor | Poor (wood burns) | 30-50 years |
| CMU block | Medium | Good | Good | 75-100 years |
| Modular prefab (steel) | High | Excellent | Excellent | 75-100 years |
| 3D printed concrete | High (40-80 MPa) | Excellent (tested) | Excellent (2hr+ fire rating) | 100+ years |
| Hempcrete (open) | Low-medium | Good | Excellent | 100+ years |

Key insight: 3D printed concrete with proper reinforcement outperforms stick-frame in every durability category.

## DIMENSION 7: SITE REQUIREMENTS

| Method | Footprint needed | Ground prep | Access constraints |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stationary gantry | Large (building + margin) | Full foundation | Can't go near edges |
| Titan (mobile) | Minimal beyond building | Slab only | Can print to edges, around obstacles |
| Crane-based | Very large (crane reach) | Full foundation | Limited reach zones |

| Aerial + ground swarm | Minimal | Minimal | Handles any terrain |

Future winner: Aerial drones for impossible terrain (cliffs, flooded areas, dense urban cores).

## DIMENSION 8: DESIGN FREEDOM

| Method | Organic forms | Cantilevers | Multi-story | Overhangs |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Conventional stick | Limited | Difficult/expensive | 3-4 max | Very expensive |
| Modular prefab | Flat panels only | Difficult | Easy (panel stacking) | Limited |
| Stationary gantry | Good | Limited by gravity | Easy | Requires support material |
| Titan | Excellent | Good | 2-3 story tested | Limited by gravity |
| Flying drones | Excellent | Excellent (no gravity on payload) | Challenging | Excellent (no support needed) |

Key insight: Flying drones eliminate the overhang problem entirely. No gravity constraint = any organic form achievable.

## DIMENSION 9: SCALABILITY

| Method | Scales by | Single machine capacity | Fleet coordination |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stationary gantry | More machines | 25-50 homes/year | None (independent) |

## DIMENSION 10: REGULATORY READINESS

| Method | Code path | Testing depth | Approval speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conventional | Fully codified | Centuries of data | Instant |
| Modular prefab | Established (IRC, IBC) | Factory certification | Weeks |
| 3D printing (all) | Alternate means + ESR | Required per system | 3-6 months per jurisdiction |
| Flying drones | No code path exists | None | Unknown (years) |

Regulatory moat is real. Flying drones have zero regulatory framework — 5-10 year gap before aerial printing is permittable in most jurisdictions.

## SYNTHESIS: BEST METHOD BY CONTEXT

| Context | Best method today | Best method 2030 |
|---|---|---|
| Mass housing (flat terrain) | Titan-style mobile gantry | Ground swarm |
| Disaster relief (remote) | Portable extruder | Flying drone swarm |
| Luxury/organic architecture | Robotic arm + human finish | Flying drones + robotic arms |
| Affordable mass housing | Stationary gantry (factory) | Ground swarm |
| Urban infill (tight sites) | Titan mobile | Aerial drone swarm |
| Remote/hostile environments | Flying drones | Flying drones |
| Off-grid/self-sufficient | Modular prefab + 3D print hybrid | Bio-based aerial print |

Winning pattern: Hybrid systems. Ground printers for structure. Aerial drones for finishing, roofing, hard-to-reach areas. Bio-based materials for carbon negativity. Active inference control for autonomous operation.
