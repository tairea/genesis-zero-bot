# 9 Edible Vines for Vertical Space

*Source: [Epic Gardening]*

Turn unused vertical space (fences, trellises, pergolas) into productive food sources. These vines produce heavy harvests with minimal effort and often return bigger each year.

---

## 1. Cucamelon (Mouse Melon)
- **Fruit:** Grape-sized, lime-flavored
- **Perennial:** In zone 7+, underground tuber returns yearly
- **Timestamp:** 0:55-1:55

## 2. Tromboncino Squash
- **Type:** Italian heirloom
- **Benefit:** Immune to squash vine borers (solid stem)
- **Growth:** Can grow 30-40 feet in a season
- **Timestamp:** 2:14-3:00

## 3. Malabar Spinach
- **Climate:** Thrives in heat/humidity when regular spinach bolts
- **Harvest:** "Cut and come again" plant
- **Timestamp:** 3:45-4:25

## 4. Scarlet Runner Bean
- **Food Sources:** Young pods, shelling beans, dry beans (3 crops!)
- **Soil:** Fixes nitrogen in soil
- **Timestamp:** 5:00-5:44

## 5. Chayote
- **Edible Parts:** Fruits, leaves, shoots, tuberous roots
- **Yield:** Single vine can produce 80-100 fruits
- **Timestamp:** 6:14-6:35

## 6. Hyacinth Bean
- **Growth:** Explosively fast
- **Pods:** Vivid purple
- **⚠️ Note:** Mature dried seeds require thorough boiling to be safe
- **Timestamp:** 7:43-8:26

## 7. Groundnut Vine
- **Type:** Native North American perennial
- **Edible:** Both tubers AND bean pods
- **Conditions:** Thrives in partial shade and moist soils
- **Timestamp:** 8:54-9:50

## 8. Passion Flower (Maypop)
- **Hardiness:** Winter-hardy to zone 5
- **Fruit:** Tropical-tasting, late summer
- **Timestamp:** 10:24-10:32

## 9. Akebia (Chocolate Vine)
- **Fruit:** Edible, vanilla-like flavor
- **Shade:** Tolerates partial shade very well
- **Timestamp:** 11:40-12:23

---

## Why These Work for Communities
- **Vertical growth = no ground space needed**
- Many are perennial = one-time planting
- High yields per square foot
- Some fix nitrogen (scarlet runner bean)
- Multiple food sources (scarlet runner, chayote)
