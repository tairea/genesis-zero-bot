# 7 Resilient Superfruits for Backyard Self-Sufficiency

*Source: [Epic Gardening / Deo]*

Commercial nurseries and big-box stores push high-maintenance, low-yield plants. These seven "superfruits" thrive with minimal care and are perfect for self-sufficiency. They're often overlooked commercially because they don't ship well.

---

## 1. Hardy Kiwi
- **Yield:** Up to 100 lbs per vine
- **Cold Hardiness:** To -25°F
- **Notes:** Unlike grapes, rarely bothered by birds or powdery mildew
- **Timestamp:** 1:04-3:22

## 2. Black Raspberries
- **Yield:** Superior to red raspberries
- **Benefits:** Higher yield, better flavor, firmer texture
- **Nutrition:** High in anthocyanins with researched anti-cancer properties
- **Timestamp:** 3:23-5:35

## 3. Sea Buckthorn
- **Nutrition:** Vitamin C, Vitamin E, Omega-7, rare B12
- **Growing:** Thrives in poor soil, hardy to -40°F
- **Harvest:** Easy when frozen in winter
- **Timestamp:** 5:36-8:17

## 4. Saskatoon Berries
- **Also called:** "The blueberry killer"
- **Yield:** 10-20 lbs per bush (much higher than blueberries)
- **Soil:** Does NOT need acidic soil
- **Timestamp:** 8:18-10:18

## 5. Sour Cherries
- **Tree:** Smaller, self-pollinating, easier to manage than sweet cherries
- **Nutrition:** High levels of melatonin (natural sleep aid)
- **Timestamp:** 10:19-12:20

## 6. Honeyberries
- **Origin:** Siberian native
- **Cold Hardiness:** Hardy to -55°F
- **Fruiting:** Very early (June)
- **Nutrition:** More antioxidants than blueberries
- **Maintenance:** Minimal
- **Timestamp:** 12:21-14:24

## 7. Aronia (Chokeberry)
- **ORAC Score:** One of the highest antioxidant scores of any berry
- **Hardiness:** Incredibly hardy, pest-resistant
- **Uses:** Juice, jams (versatile when processed)
- **Timestamp:** 14:25-16:45

---

## Why These Work for Communities
- Low maintenance = low labor burden
- High yield = good for community food share
- Resilient = reliable harvests
- Often perennial = one-time planting, years of harvest
