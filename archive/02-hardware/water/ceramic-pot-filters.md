# Ceramic Pot Water Filters

## Description
Low-tech water filtration using ceramic candles/elements (like Katadyn, Doulton, or DIY terracotta). Effective against bacteria, protozoa, and sediment.

## Source
- DIY: Terracotta candle filters
- Commercial: Katadyn, Doulton, Sawyer ceramic

## Pros
- No electricity required
- Long-lasting ceramic elements (replaceable)
- Simple to maintain
- No chemicals needed

## Cons
- Doesn't remove viruses (need UV or RO for that)
- Requires periodic cleaning
- Slow flow rate
- Brittle elements can crack

## Cost Range
- DIY: $10-30 (terracotta pot + activated charcoal)
- Commercial: $50-200

## Best For
- Off-grid communities
- Emergency preparedness
- Remote locations
- Budget-conscious implementations

## DIY Guide
1. Terracotta pot + activated charcoal + sediment layer
2. Clean ceramic candle weekly
3. Replace charcoal monthly
