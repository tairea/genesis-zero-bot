## MUTATION LOG: OPTIMIZATION PARAMETERS

**What changed from original and why:**

- Original: Hydro-primary (dependent on specific topography)
- Mutated original: Solar + hydrogen (dependent on hydrogen supply chain)
- Optimized: DIVERSE STACK - micro-hydro + solar + wind + bio-gas + battery
- Reason: No single generation source is reliable under all conditions. Diversity is resilience.

**MANUFACTURING:**
- Original: E-waste primary (dependent on external electronics supply)
- Mutated original: Mycelium + agave (dependent on specific desert biology)
- Optimized: MULTI-STREAM CIRCULAR - e-waste + timber + bamboo + bio-char + metal recycling
- Reason: Every material stream has failure modes. Combined, they cover for each other.

**GOVERNANCE:**
- Original: Proposal graph (efficient but tech-dependent)
- Mutated original: Sociocracy (legitimate but slow)
- Optimized: NESTED COUNCILS + PROPOSAL GRAPH HYBRID
- Reason: Small daily decisions should be fast. Large strategic decisions should be legitimate.

**WATER:**
- Original: Rainwater + greywater reed beds (temperate)
- Mutated original: Groundwater + RO desalination (desert)
- Optimized: RAINWATER + STREAM INTAKE + WETLAND FILTRATION + ATMOSPHERIC
- Reason: Redundancy at every stage.

**SHELTER:**
- Original: Earth-sheltered (3-6 month build time)
- Mutated original: Shipping containers (fast but industrial)
- Optimized: CORDWOOD MASONRY + STRAW BALE + BAMBOO FRAME
- Reason: Fastest to habitability (2-4 weeks for basic shell). Lowest skill barrier.

**FOOD:**
- Original: Community garden (20% of diet)
- Mutated original: Food forest (50% eventually)
- Optimized: PERMACULTURE FOOD FOREST + CHICKEN RABBITRY + MUSHROOM LOGS + SOLAR DRIED FOODS
- Reason: 80% of diet from perennial systems by year 3 with minimal labor once established.

**MOBILITY:**
- Original: EV pool (dependent on battery supply)
- Mutated original: E-bikes + animal power (limited range)
- Optimized: WALKING + CARGO BIKES + SAILING CANOE + SMALL EV POOL
- Reason: In a dense walkable settlement, 80% of trips are under 3km. Most communities over-build mobility and under-build walkability.

## 1. SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

```
Top layer: NESTED COUNCIL GOVERNANCE + PROPOSAL GRAPH
- Household circle: Daily decisions (24-hour turnaround)
- Village council: Weekly decisions (3-5 day turnaround)
- Network council: Monthly decisions (seasonal planning)
- Proposal graph: Information substrate for all councils

Five operational layers:

ENERGY LAYER: Micro-hydro + solar + wind + bio-gas + battery (5 sources)
COMMUNICATION LAYER: Mesh network + paper archives + in-person meetings
MANUFACTURING LAYER: Timber + bamboo + e-waste + bio-char + metal recycling (5 streams)
SHELTER LAYER: Cordwood + straw bale + bamboo + earth ovens (4 systems)
MOBILITY LAYER: Walking + cargo bikes + sailing canoe + small EV pool

Base layer: FOOD + WATER

WATER LAYER: Rainwater + stream intake + wetland filtration + atmospheric water
FOOD LAYER: Permaculture forest + chicken/rabbitry + mushroom logs + solar drying
```

**KEY DESIGN PRINCIPLE:**
Every layer has at least 3 independent input sources. Every critical system has a manual fallback. Every decision has a clear council level. No single point of failure can collapse the community.

## 2. ENERGY LAYER

**FIVE INDEPENDENT GENERATION SOURCES:**

1. MICRO-HYDRO (primary baseload): 3-6 kW continuous from stream/creek, 24/7 when water flows
2. SOLAR PV (peak daytime): 4-6 kW from salvaged panels, daytime loads
3. WIND (supplementary): 1-2 kW from VAWT, quiet and bird-safe
4. BIO-GAS (always available): 2-3 kW from kitchen waste + garden waste + manure
5. BATTERY STORAGE (emergency buffer): 15-20 kWh from salvaged EV packs

**TOTAL:** 10-14 kW continuous average, 20+ kWh storage
**COVERS:** 10-person community comfortably

**Energy balance:**
- Generation: ~40-60 kWh/day
- Storage: 15-20 kWh battery + 500L hot water thermal
- Load: 8-12 kWh/day
- Margin: 30-50 kWh/day for manufacturing and transport

## 3. WATER LAYER

**FOUR INDEPENDENT SOURCES:**

1. RAINWATER HARVESTING (primary): 500 sq ft roof per person = 500 gallons per inch rain. 10,000 gallon ferro-cement tank. Filtration: sediment + carbon + UV.
2. STREAM INTAKE (secondary): Small infiltration intake. Sand/media filter + UV. 500-2,000 gallons/day.
3. CONSTRUCTED WETLAND (tertiary): 100 sq ft per person. Processes greywater. Output: irrigation water. Bonus: Edible plants and fish.
4. ATMOSPHERIC WATER GENERATION (emergency): Solar stills, 10 sq ft per person, 3-5 gallons/day. Never fails if you have sun.

**TOTAL:** 1,500-3,000 gallons/day safe supply per 10-person community (10x conventional use)

**Water use budget:** ~25 gallons/person/day (vs 80-100 conventional)

## 4. FOOD LAYER

**FOUR PRODUCTION SYSTEMS:**

1. PERMACULTURE FOOD FOREST (perennial staples, year 3+): 7-layer forest system (canopy to roots). Nitrogen fixers throughout. 80% of caloric needs by year 5. 2 hours/week per person once established.

2. CHICKEN + RABBITRY (protein, 6 months to production): 10 chickens per person (eggs + meat). 5 rabbits per person (meat + pelts). Free-range in food forest. Manure -> compost -> food forest.

3. MUSHROOM LOGS (protein, 6 weeks to first harvest): Shiitake on logs, oyster on straw substrate. 2-4 lbs per log per year. Spent substrate -> compost.

4. SOLAR DRYING + FERMENTATION (preservation, zero energy): Solar dryers from reclaimed windows. Fermentation: kimchi, sauerkraut, kombucha. 6-12 months shelf life without refrigeration.

**Food budget (year 3+):**
- 70-80% from food forest
- 10-15% from animals
- 5-10% from mushrooms
- 5% from preserved surplus

**LABOR:** 8-12 hours/week per person including processing and preservation

## 5. SHELTER LAYER

**CORDWOOD MASONRY + STRAW BALE**

**Cordwood masonry:** Log slices (4-6" diameter) in mortar. Source: Thinning from land clearing. Thermal mass excellent. Cost: $0.50-1.00/sq ft. Skill barrier: Low. Build time: Basic 400 sq ft shell in 2-3 weeks.

**Straw bale:** Straw bales as wall insulation ($5-10 each). R-50+ insulation. Fire risk low with plaster. Build time: 1 week roof structure + 1 week bale installation.

**Bamboo frame:** From local stand. Tensile strength comparable to steel. Roof structure, porches, furniture.

**Earth ovens + rocket mass heaters:** Built from clay, sand, brick, scrap metal. $50-200 in materials. 12+ hours of warmth from single fire.

**BUILD SEQUENCE:**
- Week 1: Site clearing, bamboo frame, roof structure
- Week 2: Cordwood walls
- Week 3: Straw bale roof, plastering
- Week 4: Earth oven, plumbing, electrical

**TIME TO HABITABILITY:** 3-4 weeks (vs 3-6 months earth-sheltered)
**SHELL COST:** $2,000-5,000 for 400 sq ft (materials only)

## 6. GOVERNANCE LAYER

**NESTED COUNCILS + PROPOSAL GRAPH**

**HOUSEHOLD COUNCIL:** Members sharing a home. Weekly 30-min meeting. Decisions: meals, chores, guest policy, purchases under $50. Turnaround: 24 hours.

**VILLAGE COUNCIL:** One representative from each household. Weekly 1-hour meeting. Decisions: land use, shared infrastructure, labor, resources under $500. Turnaround: 3-5 days.

**NETWORK COUNCIL:** One delegate from each village. Monthly 2-hour meeting. Decisions: trade, shared manufacturing, new village founding. Turnaround: 1 month.

**PROPOSAL GRAPH FUNCTIONS:**
1. Decision memory: Full history of every council decision
2. Information broker: Connects villages with shared knowledge
3. Workflow tracking: Tasks from large decisions
4. Resonance detection: Surfaces emerging needs before crisis

**CONSENT PRINCIPLE:** Any member can block with a concern. Must propose alternative. If no alternative, delay until one emerges.

## 7. MATERIALS LAYER

**FIVE CIRCULAR STREAMS:**

1. **TIMBER** (from land clearing): Cordwood, beams, furniture, charcoal. Sawdust -> mushroom substrate; branches -> bio-char; bark -> compost. Cost: $0.

2. **BAMBOO** (local stand): Structural framing, flooring, furniture, piping, fiber. 30% annual selective harvest. Matures in 3-5 years. Cost: $0 once established.

3. **E-WASTE + SALVAGE**: Motors, sensors, wire, PCBs for repair, copper. Bridge material, not primary source. Cost: $0-100/year.

4. **BIO-CHAR + BIO-PLASTIC** (agricultural waste): Flame cap kiln -> bio-char for soil/water filtration. Alkali treatment -> bioplastic sheet. Cost: $0.

5. **METAL RECYCLING** (salvage): Steel structural, brackets, fasteners. Cutting torch + welding. Cost: $100-500/year.

## 8. MOBILITY LAYER

**WALKING-FIRST DESIGN**

**WALKING + HIKING PATHS:** 3m wide, connecting every structure. 5 minutes to kitchen garden, common house, work areas. Cost: $500-1,000 per 100m.

**CARGO BIKES:** Front-loading cargo bike. 30-50 km range. $300-600 to build. For children, groceries, tools, small loads.

**SAILING CANOE:** 6m craft. 500kg cargo + 4 passengers. Wind + paddle. $500-1,500 to build. For rivers, coastal, market runs.

**SMALL EV POOL:** 1 pickup per 10 people (salvage EV conversion). 80-120 km range. Solar carport charging. Medical emergencies, bulk runs, construction materials. $3,000-5,000 per conversion.

**DESIGN PRINCIPLE:** If the settlement is walkable (everything within 15 minutes), the EV pool is rarely needed.
