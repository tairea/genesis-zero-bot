## 2. ENERGY LAYER (MUTATED)

### Generation

**Solar primary:** 15-20 kW peak (desert: 6+ sun hours/day, near-zero cloud cover)
- Bifacial panels on steel frames (salvaged mining equipment)
- East-west split: morning panels (east-facing) + afternoon panels (west-facing) for extended generation
- Tracking mounts from car steering racks + linear actuators
- Dust cleaning: automated compressed air blasts twice daily

**Wind supplement:** 2-4 kW average
- Vertical-axis wind turbine (VAWT) from salvaged HVAC fans + 3D-printed blades
- Desert wind is reliable: afternoon thermal winds + seasonal corridor winds

**Hydrogen storage:** 50 kWh equivalent (the mutation that changes everything)
- Electrolyzer: 2-4 kW unit powered by excess solar
- Hydrogen storage: pressurized tanks (salvaged industrial gas bottles)
- Fuel cell: 1-2 kW unit for overnight/emergency power
- Why hydrogen: Desert heat degrades lithium batteries. Hydrogen tolerates 50C+. Enables high-temperature industrial processes (kiln, forge).

**Biogas backup:** 1-2 kW
- Agave waste + cactus waste + food scraps
- 2-3m tall digester tank, fed weekly

### Storage

- Hydrogen system: 50 kWh equivalent (2-day autonomy)
- Hot water tanks: 500L insulated
- Phase-change salt hydrate (Glauber's salt): 58C thermal storage

### Critical Assumption

Site has groundwater access (bore well, 30-100m depth). If not, desalination from brackish water triples energy cost for water. Budget $15,000-30,000 for well drilling.

## 4. MANUFACTURING LAYER (MUTATED)

### Biological Materials Chain

**Agave/Cactus (primary feedstock):**
- Agave Americana: Fast-growing (5-7 years), drought-tolerant
- Biomass -> bio-plastic through alkali treatment (lye + heat)
- Bio-plastic -> filament for 3D printing OR compression molding
- Waste: 5-10% becomes high-nitrogen fertilizer

**Mycelium composites (replaces plastic e-waste filament):**
- Substrate: Agricultural waste (agave pulp, cactus waste, straw)
- Inoculation: Oyster mushroom spawn
- Growth: 7-10 days in molds at ambient desert temperatures (25-35C)
- Products: Insulation panels, packaging, structural non-load-bearing parts, furniture
- Fire-resistant, fully compostable, R-4/inch

**Desert limestone:**
- Quarry local limestone (within 5km)
- Kiln (solar concentrator or waste-heat) -> quicklime -> lime putty -> construction materials

**Salvaged containers (structural framework):**
- 20ft and 40ft shipping containers ($1,000-3,000 each)
- Modification: Cut openings, weld steel frames, insulate with mycelium panels
- Advantage: 6 weeks to install vs 3-6 months for earth-sheltered

- Evaporative cooler + solar fan

**Stage 1 (Month 3-9):** Container home cluster
- 2-3 connected containers per household
- Earth berms on north and west walls (passive cooling: 10-15C cooler inside than outside)
- Courtyard layout: Containers around shaded outdoor space

**Stage 2 (Month 9-24):** Community campus
- Multiple households around shared courtyards
- Desert gardening: Agave, cactus, shade trees for passive cooling
- Underground thermal mass for heat storage

### Water System

- Groundwater: Bore well, 30-100m depth
- Desalination: If brackish, small RO unit ($3,000-5,000)
- Greywater: Direct to food forest via swales
- Water use: 20-30 gallons/person/day (vs 50-80 conventional)

### Critical Assumption

Groundwater accessible and not too saline. If bore well yields nothing at 100m, entire design fails. Requires 3-6 months of water exploration before site commitment.

## 10. CRITICAL GAPS (MUTATED)

**Gap 1: Water security (#1 priority)**
Desert water is the existential constraint. Groundwater depletion is real. Site selection within 50km of reliable aquifer. Investigate qanats, hauz, small dams. Consider treated greywater for mycelium and food forest. 6-12 months of water exploration before site commitment.

**Gap 2: Hydrogen system reliability**
Hydrogen systems are complex. Start with smaller system (5-10 kWh equivalent) to prove loop before 50 kWh. R&D needed: Solar-powered electrolysis controller, hydrogen leak detection, safe pressurized storage in extreme heat.

**Gap 3: Mycelium scaling**
Proven at small scale but not at full building insulation scale. Design modular growing chambers for replication. Long-term durability testing needed (humidity cycling, desert dust, temperature extremes).

**Gap 4: Bio-plastic production yield**
Agave bio-plastic proven in labs but not community scale. UV stabilizer needed for outdoor exposure. Natural stabilizers (turmeric, charcoal) may work.

**Gap 5: Animal welfare in extreme heat**
Design shade structures + mister systems. Rotate work to early morning/evening in summer. Sociocracy governance must enforce seasonal work limits.

**Gap 6: Summer rest period enforcement**
Mandatory rest 2-5pm in summer. Economic pressure may push people to work through it. Governance circle is responsible for enforcing seasonal rhythm.

**Gap 7: Shipping container availability**
Prices fluctuate wildly ($1,000-5,000). Establish relationships with ports/salvage yards before Phase 1. Fallback: steel frame + mycelium panel construction.
