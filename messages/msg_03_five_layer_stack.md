🌿⚡ **MESSAGE 3: THE FIVE-LAYER STACK**

The system is organized in 5 layers. Each layer solves specific contradictions and creates new ones. None can exist without the layer below; each is constrained by what the layer above demands.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  LAYER 5: KNOWLEDGE                      │ ← What gets remembered
│  LAYER 4: ECONOMIC                        │ ← What flows without money
│  LAYER 3: COORDINATION                    │ ← Control, ECS, sensing
│  LAYER 2: COMPUTATION                     │ ← Relay/tube/transistor
│  LAYER 1: PHYSICAL                       │ ← What you can touch
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**LAYER 1: PHYSICAL SUBSTRATE**
What it is: Stone, wood, metal, clay, fiber. Tools, shelters, water systems, roads. Anything you can touch with your hands.

What it resolves:
- S6: Short-term survival (immediate needs handled here)
- P3 partial: Social continuity has physical anchors (meeting halls, communal kitchens)
- S1 partial: Mechanical computation lives here

What it creates:
- The material constraints that shape everything above (you can't compute what you can't build)
- Entropy (everything degrades without maintenance labor)

**LAYER 2: COMPUTATIONAL SUBSTRATE**
What it is: Relays → vacuum tubes → discrete transistors → ICs. The stuff that does logic.

What it resolves:
- S1: Technical complexity vs local manufacturability (each stage uses tools from the previous stage)
- S5: Stability vs adaptability (ECS formal model allows dynamic reconfiguration without hardware change)

What it creates:
- The bootstrapping problem (Stage N requires Stage N-1's tools, which requires Stage N-2's...)
- Dependencies that can span centuries

**LAYER 3: COORDINATION SUBSTRATE**
What it is: Control loops, sensing networks, ECS component systems, the software that runs on Layer 2 hardware.

What it resolves:
- S3: Centralized vs distributed coordination (ECS handles this — components don't care about physical location)
- P2 partial: Autonomy preserved at entity level; coordination happens at system level
- S5: Stable AND adaptable (stable components, adaptive system configuration)

What it creates:
- Complexity that requires expertise to maintain (contradiction: expertise = single points of failure)
- Information overload at scale (more sensing = more decisions needed)

**LAYER 4: ECONOMIC SUBSTRATE**
What it is: Gift economy at village level → labor vouchers at inter-village level → energy accounting at regional level. Three-tier non-monetary system.

What it resolves:
- P1: Local self-sufficiency vs inter-community needs (three tiers cover all scales)
- S2: Gift economy scale limits (tiers prevent gift from having to scale)
- S7: Unpleasant work (rotation + social status + mechanical substitution)
- P2 partial: Autonomy preserved (no one tells you what to produce), coordination achieved (resource flows visible)

What it creates:
- Social debt ledger complexity (who owes what to whom across villages)
- The risk of vouchers becoming "money-like" over time (requires constant vigilance)
- Specialization temptation (once you have vouchers, you might want to accumulate them)

**LAYER 5: KNOWLEDGE SUBSTRATE**
What it is: Ceramic plates, mechanical devices, microfilm, biological cultures, oral traditions — five encoding modes that back each other up.

What it resolves:
- S4: Knowledge durability vs accessibility (each mode has different failure modes, so degradation in one triggers reconstruction from others)
- P3 partial: Social continuity (values, procedures, history survive personnel turnover)

What it creates:
- The knowledge transmission problem (how do you ensure the next generation actually learns?)
- The interpretation problem (how do you ensure meaning survives?)
- What gets remembered vs. what gets lost (selection bias)

**KEY INTERFACE: LAYER 4 ↔ LAYER 5**
This is where the hardest contradictions live:
- Economic flows (labor vouchers, energy) fund knowledge storage (ceramic encoding, apprenticeship time)
- Knowledge substrates (what's remembered) shape economic behaviors (what's valued)
- Break this interface, and both layers collapse

**THE ASCENDING DEPENDENCY:**
Each layer depends on the layer below for existence, and constrains the layer above for direction.

L5 (knowledge) → L4 (economic) → L3 (coordination) → L2 (computation) → L1 (physical)
                     ↑
               These flows go UP
            (demands, constraints)
                     ↓
              These flows go DOWN
            (resources, substrates)
