## Headline Findings

### 1. The Funding Gap Is Critical
Regenerative agriculture attracts billions in institutional capital (TIAA: 2M acres; SLM Partners: $760M AUM; US sustainable farmland: $50B+). **Village-scale residential communities have no equivalent.** The largest named village-specific raise in the entire 2016–2026 period is Geoship's $21M equity crowdfunding.

### 2. The Largest Verified Village Raises
- **Geoship** (bioceramic dome villages, CA): **$21M+** from ~3,958 investors via Wefunder/Republic (2020–ongoing)
- **Arcata 30th Street Commons** (tribal affordable housing, CA): **$11.4M** state grant (2021–2023)
- **Cairnspring Mills** (regenerative community mill, OR): **~$2M** community equity + **$35M debt** (2025)
- **Drewsteignton Community Society** (UK village pub): **£555,000** via community shares (2023)
- **ReGen Villages** (James Ehrlich, Stanford spin-off): **€350K** seed (2016)

### 3. The Investor Landscape Is Thin
Only **Wild Community Impact Fund** (Australia, targeting $100M AUD) is attempting to build an institutional-grade village property trust. Most village financing relies on: community shares (UK), CDFI loans (US), government grants (EU LEADER up to €500K), and member self-funding.

### 4. Key Investors With Deal People
- **SLM Partners** ($760M AUM): Brendan Costello, Mike Tolli — regenerative farmland, no village product
- **Iroquois Valley REIT** ($126M AUM): Christopher Zuehlsdorff (CEO) — farmland REIT, no village product
- **Dirt Capital** ($30M+ fund): Jacob Israelow (Founder) — regenerative farmland, no village product
- **RSF Social Finance** ($1B+ cumulative): Michael Jones (VP Lending BD) — community lending, no village product
- **Gen-Re**: Koen van Seijen (Founding Partner) — agriculture SPV syndicate, no village equivalent

### 5. The Seven Structural Gaps (What Exists for Farms But NOT Villages)
1. **No institutional village real assets fund** (equivalent to SLM/TIAA farmland)
2. **No community lending platform for village infrastructure** (Steward = farms; nothing equivalent for villages)
3. **No carbon/ecosystem services revenue model** (farmland has verified carbon credits; villages don't)
4. **No blended concessional capital fund for village pilots** (Dirt Capital model, but for villages)
5. **No SPV syndicate platform** (Gen-Re democratizes ag deals at $1K; no village equivalent)
6. **No public REIT for regenerative neighborhoods** (Iroquois Valley proves the model; none exist for villages)
7. **No pension fund awareness/education infrastructure** for villages as institutional asset class

### 6. The Most Accessible Funding Sources Today
- **EU LEADER Programme** (Ireland/Scotland): Up to €500K per community project, 75% grant rate
- **California Climate Investments / AHSC**: State grants for sustainable community development
- **UK Community Shares**: £200M+ raised cumulatively; EIS tax relief; Drewsteignton (£555K) as proof of concept
- **Equity Crowdfunding**: Geoship ($21M) and Cairnspring Mills prove demand exists; platforms (Wefunder, Republic) are ready
- **CDFIs**: Community Housing Partners, Enterprise — but focused on affordable housing, not regenerative villages

### 7. Confidence Rating
**Medium.** Village financing data is poorly tracked. Many projects are member-funded without public disclosure. The figures above represent only verified, named sources. True total flows are significantly undercounted.
